# Welcome to EXPLORE at RIT! 🤖🌊

## Who We Are
Hey there! We're EXPLORE at RIT, and we're pretty excited about what we're building here. We bring together students from all kinds of majors - whether you're deep into engineering or just curious about robotics - because honestly, the best ideas come from diverse perspectives.

## What Gets Us Up Every Morning
We're on a mission to make waves in environmental engineering, literally! We're focusing on maritime monitoring and exploration to build robotics that actually contribute to science. Our big dream? To be the first robotics club at RIT to actively participate in field research, exploring and recording local aquatic environments and wildlife. 

But here's the thing - we're doing this sustainably. We're not just talking about helping the environment; we're walking the walk by using readily available parts and recyclable materials to craft our boat buoys and underwater tech.

## What We're Actually Building
Right now, we're working on some pretty cool stuff:

🛠️ **Our Current Project**: We've got this boat that projects a camera feed via wireless connection - it's our exhibit piece that shows off what's possible with low-cost, sustainable materials. But that's just the beginning.

🌊 **The Big Picture**: We want to expand the range and vertical reach of our remote vessels. Our ultimate goal? To develop capabilities sufficient enough to explore Lake Ontario, mapping the lake's ground structure and wildlife populations. 

🔬 **The Process**: Through each iteration, our builds are becoming increasingly refined. We're aiming for something comparable to industry standards currently used in ocean exploration research - but with our sustainable, accessible approach.

We're all about showing how remote scouting features can be practically applied for environmental purposes. It's not just about the tech; it's about proving that persistent and genuine efforts can reach even the deepest depths of our oceans.

## Want to Jump In?
Whether you're into robotics, care about the environment, or just think underwater robots are awesome, we'd love to have you!
